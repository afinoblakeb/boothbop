# BoothBop 0.0.4 Feature Brief: Social-Ready Sharing

## Release

- Candidate version: `0.0.4`
- Owner: BoothBop
- Decision date: 2026-07-21
- Target evaluation date: 2026-07-21

## Consumer Problem

An animated BoothBop result fails at the end of the core journey when someone
selects a social app that does not accept the shared GIF file.

## Evidence

- User feedback: In-person use with a group identified social sharing as the
  clearest missing part of the experience.
- Product observation: Selecting Instagram after sharing a GIF produces an
  incompatible handoff instead of a postable animation.
- Frequency or severity: Sharing is a primary completion action and the failure
  affects every GIF session intended for a video-oriented social app.
- Why this problem matters now: A failed final action wastes the complete booth
  session and prevents the output from spreading to other potential users.

## Proposed Capability

When GIF is selected, the primary Share Animation action creates an on-device
H.264 MP4 that exactly preserves the GIF's frames, Look, Boom setting, Boom
speed, and branding preference, then opens the normal iOS share sheet. The
animation is centered on a 9:16 canvas inside the 4:5 feed-safe region so the
same file remains visible when Instagram offers Post, Reel, or Story.
The original GIF remains available through a secondary Share Original GIF
action. Strip and Video sharing retain their existing media types.

The release also includes a single dismissible, version-aware in-app notice on
the home screen so people who received an automatic update can discover the new
sharing behavior. This is launch education for the approved capability, not a
notification center or a background push-notification system.

## Simpler Alternatives Considered

1. Rename GIF to Video. Rejected because GIF remains useful in Messages and the
   web, and removing it would not preserve the output users selected.
2. Tell users to switch to the Video tab before sharing. Rejected because this
   makes users understand a file compatibility problem the app can solve.
3. Use an undocumented Instagram URL scheme. Rejected because it is
   destination-specific, brittle, and would not improve sharing to other apps.

## Non-Goals

- Direct publishing, authentication, or integration with a social network SDK.
- Custom aspect-ratio, caption, hashtag, music, or branded end-card editors.
- Uploads, analytics, accounts, or server-side transcoding.
- Changes to capture, the classic strip, GIF preview, or the Video tab output.

## User Journey

The user completes the existing four-photo flow, selects GIF, and sees the same
animated preview as today. Tapping Share Animation immediately shows a
Preparing status, renders a compatible MP4 locally, and opens the iOS share
sheet. Dismissing the sheet returns to the unchanged review screen. If
preparation fails, the app explains the failure and leaves Share Original GIF
available. Subsequent shares reuse the prepared MP4 until an output choice
changes.

## Required States

- First use: Sharing requires no setup, account, permission, or explanation.
- Repeat use: A prepared social video is reused without another encode.
- Loading: The primary action says Preparing and is disabled against repeats.
- Empty: The action remains disabled until the selected output exists.
- Error: Social-video failure is visible and never silently shares a GIF.
- Permission denied: No new permission is required; share-sheet denial is
  reported using the existing error treatment.
- Offline: Generation and sharing remain entirely on device.
- Compact phone: Preview, primary Share action, original-GIF fallback, and Start
  Over remain reachable at 320x568 without horizontal overflow.
- Large phone: The current centered review composition remains unchanged.
- Accessibility: Labels identify the exported medium and preparation status is
  announced through a live region. The update notice has a named dismiss action.

## Success Criteria

- Behavioral: GIF's primary action shares a `.mp4` with MIME `video/mp4`; Share
  Original GIF shares the unchanged `.gif`.
- Quality: Social video matches GIF frame order, Look, Boom timing, and branding.
- Compatibility: H.264 MP4 at 1080x1920 and 30 FPS, with the complete 1080px
  square composition contained inside the centered 1080x1350 feed-safe crop.
- Performance: Preparation starts visibly within one frame and completes within
  3 seconds on the largest supported simulator; repeat sharing is immediate.
- TestFlight feedback: Instagram accepts the animation without a compatibility
  error, and Messages and Save Video preserve motion and visual quality.

## Removal Criteria

Hold or remove the capability if the converted animation is visually different
from the preview, if preparation regularly exceeds 3 seconds, if Instagram
still rejects the output, or if the additional GIF fallback crowds the compact
review screen.

## Test Plan

- Unit coverage: Share policy, frame ordering, timing, duration, resolution
  floor, format, and filename.
- Browser journey: Strip/GIF/Video labels; GIF primary MP4 handoff; original GIF
  fallback; Boom and branding invalidation; failure and cancellation states;
  update-notice first display, dismissal, and persistence.
- Native simulator: Clean-install launch and full core flow on the compact,
  standard, and largest installed iPhone simulators.
- Media inspection: Assert MP4 container, H.264 codec, 1080x1920 dimensions,
  cream top/bottom margins, visible center content, duration, and no unexpected
  audio stream.
- Physical clean install: Share GIF animation to Instagram, Messages, and Save
  Video once through TestFlight.
- Physical update install: Upgrade from public 0.0.3 and repeat the share flow.
- Airplane Mode: Complete capture, MP4 preparation, and opening the share sheet.

## Prototype Reuse

- `src/lib/videoNative.ts`: existing on-device AVAssetWriter adapter.
- `ios/App/App/AppDelegate.swift`: existing H.264 MP4 writer.
- `src/lib/session.ts`: existing normal and Boom frame ordering.
- `src/lib/watermark.ts`: existing optional BoothBop branding.
