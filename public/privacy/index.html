Privacy Policy

BoothBop · Last updated June 23, 2026

BoothBop is a photo booth that runs entirely on your device. It is private by design.

The BoothBop app collects no data. The sections below about our website and our support email describe activity that happens outside the app — not within it.

The short version

We do not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. Your photos and videos never leave your device. There are no accounts, no servers, and no cloud. We use no analytics, tracking, advertising, or third-party SDKs.

Camera

BoothBop uses your device's camera only to capture the photos you choose to take. The camera feed and the resulting photos are processed locally on your device and are never uploaded anywhere.

Your photos

The photos, strips, GIFs, and videos you create are stored locally on your device (in on-device storage, and in your photo library if you choose to save them). They remain under your control. You can delete them from BoothBop's own gallery at any time, or from your device's Photos app.

Data we collect

None. The BoothBop app has no backend and no accounts, so there is nothing for us to collect about you through the app.

Hosting (website only)

Our website at boothbop.com is served as static files by GitHub Pages. As with any website, the host may automatically log standard technical request information (such as IP address and browser type) for security and operational purposes. We do not access or use this information to track you. This applies only to the website — the installed app loads entirely from its bundled files and makes no such requests, so it logs nothing.

Support email

If you email support@boothbop.com, we receive your message and email address solely to respond to you. We do not use it for anything else. This happens through your email app and our inbox — not through the BoothBop app, which collects nothing.

Children

BoothBop is safe for all ages and does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13. Because there are no accounts, no servers, and no data collection, there is nothing for anyone to share with us. Photos a child takes stay on the device, under the device owner's control.

Changes

If we update this policy, we will revise the date above. Continued use of BoothBop means you accept the current policy.

Contact

Questions about your privacy? Email support@boothbop.com.
